Paring Sessions for Newbies!

by Kara and Darrin

What is Pair Programming?

Pair programming is the process of programming alongside another person, which we call Pairing. It is heavily used by Developers, especially at Dev Bootcamp. To pair with someone, you:

  1. Check-in
  2. Discuss your workflow style
  3. Decide who will Drive and who will Navigate first
  4. Switch roles
  5. Give Feedback
  6. *FISTBUMP* at your success!

Why is pair programming important to DBC?

Pair programming, or "pairing", is important for various reasons:

  • Pairing with another person gives you another set of eyes on your code, aleviating you from having many errors pop-up from having tunnel vision as you write your code.
  • Pairing allows for different perspectives that help you contribute a broad set of knowledge to your code.
  • When you pair with another programmer, having the opoprtunity to discuss your code can build understanding about your coding style, and help move you forward when you get stuck
  • Working in teams is fun, and useful! Having a culture that promotes pair programming also breaks the "lone coder in the corner" stereotype many people have about developers.

Driving and Navigating, what's that?

Driving and Navigating are the two roles you use when pairing.

The driver's role is:

  • To type the code out
  • To keep an eye on the syntax
  • To push the programming process forward

The navigator's role is:

  • To Keep an eye on the flow of the programming, and where the pair is going
  • To research and relay the information to the driver
  • To keep a step back from the code, and see how the code is working
